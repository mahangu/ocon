NEW DELHI, January 27: Indian External Affairs Minister Pranab Mukherjee is arriving in Colombo this evening to hold "frank and constructive" talks with President Mahinda Rajapaksa on important matters of mutual interest.

Before leaving for Colombo, Mukherjee spoke to Tamil Nadu Chief Minister Muthuvel Karunanidhi, now in a Chennai hospital recovering from a back ailment. Karunanidhi has requested

The 85-year-old Karunanidhi wants India to make efforts for a ceasefire in the northern province so that innocent Tamil civilians do not suffer in the ongoing clashes between Tamil Tigers and Sri Lankan security forces.

Speaking to reporters before leaving for Colombo, Mukherjee made it clear that India has no sympathy for the Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam (LTTE). However, he said that civilian Tamils have to be saved from becoming victims of the conflict in the island nation.

"We are for a fight against terrorists and all sorts of terrorism. Therefore, we have no sympathy for any terrorist activity indulged in by any organisation, particularly LTTE, which is a banned organisation in India," he declared.

At the same time, Mukherjee said India is concerned over the plight of civilians. "We have to see how civilians can be protected. They should not become hapless victims of the situation."
