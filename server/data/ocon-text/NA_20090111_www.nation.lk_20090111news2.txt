NewsThe EU Heads of Mission in Colombo referring to the significant military developments in the north of Sri Lanka, over recent days has urged that these developments pave the way towards a sustainable and equitable peace. 



 In a statement issued in this regard on Friday, the EU Heads of Mission said they therefore call upon all parties to work vigorously towards a political solution, which addresses the legitimate concerns of all communities in Sri Lanka. 



 "Only this will provide a foundation on which the people of Sri Lanka can live in peace and prosperity. EU Heads of Mission also call upon the Government and other parties to minimise the impact of the conflict on the civilian population and civil society, in particular by ensuring that humanitarian needs in the north are properly assessed and met, and by ensuring that civil society is able to operate without impediment. 



 "In particular ED Heads of Mission strongly condemn the repeated attacks on and intimidation of the media that have occurred over recent months, including the armed attack on the MTV TV-station and the brutal assassination of The Sunday Leader's Chief Editor, and urge the Sri Lankan authorities to take all necessary steps to the bring the perpetrators to justice."
