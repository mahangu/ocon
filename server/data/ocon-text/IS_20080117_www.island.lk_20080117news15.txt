Minister of Healthcare and Nutrition, Nimal Siripala de Silva left for Geneva yesterday to attend the World Health Organization executive board meeting on budget and administration for the year 2008.

The two day meeting will begin today and will be attended by a number of countries including Libya, Male, Mexico, USA, Afghanistan, Denmark, Portugal, Sri Lanka, Japan, Singapore and El Salvador.

The Health Minister will seek WHO assistance for non communicable disease control programme in Sri Lanka for 2008, which is the only country in South East Asia that provides free healthcare facilities to the entire population, Ministry sources said.

The role played by the Ministry of Healthcare in controlling communicable diseases has been commended by the WHO and is expected to provide financial and technical support to further develop the health sector. (SJ)
