T56 stolen from PC on election duty

A T56 weapon of a policeman deployed on election duty with 40 rounds of ammunition was stolen at a polling station in the Anamaduwa electorate on Monday night, Anamduwa police said.

The theft had taken place at the Kottagama polling station.

Two policemen had accompanied the election staff with the ballot boxes from the Puttalam Kachcheri to the Kottagama polling station in the evening of January 25 and spent the night at the polling station.

It was found that the T56 weapon and 40 rounds of ammunition issued to one of the two policemen had gone missing during the night, Anamaduwas Police said.

However, the polling in the area was peaceful and incident free and special police and Navy patrols were deployed in the area.
